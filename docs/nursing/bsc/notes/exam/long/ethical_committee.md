# Ethical Committee

## Definition
An **Ethical Committee** (also called **Ethics Committee** or **Institutional Review Board**) is a group of experts and community members who review and guide ethical decisions in healthcare and research.

## Key Points
- It protects the rights, safety, and welfare of patients and research participants.
- It reviews research proposals before they start.
- It helps solve ethical dilemmas in patient care.
- It makes sure laws and ethical standards are followed.

## Detailed Explanation

### Purpose
1. **Protect Patients** – Ensures patients are treated fairly and safely.
2. **Review Research** – Checks that research involving humans or animals is ethical.
3. **Guidance** – Gives advice on ethical problems in healthcare.
4. **Education** – Teaches staff about ethics and patient rights.

### Composition
An ethical committee usually includes:
- Chairperson (senior doctor or professor)
- Medical doctors
- Nurses
- Pharmacists
- Lawyers
- Social workers
- Religious or spiritual leaders
- Community representatives
- Lay persons (non-medical public members)

### Functions
- Approve or reject research proposals
- Monitor ongoing research
- Review informed consent forms
- Investigate complaints about ethical violations
- Advise on end-of-life decisions
- Help in organ donation and transplantation ethics
- Make policies for ethical patient care

### Types of Committees
1. **Institutional Ethics Committee (IEC)** – For hospitals and institutions.
2. **Research Ethics Committee (REC)** – For research projects.
3. **Clinical Ethics Committee** – For clinical care decisions.

## Bullet Summary
- Ethical Committee = group that reviews ethics in healthcare/research
- Members: doctors, nurses, lawyers, social workers, community members
- Functions: protect patients, review research, solve dilemmas, educate staff
- Types: IEC, REC, Clinical Ethics Committee

## Example
A hospital wants to test a new cancer medicine. The Ethical Committee reviews the trial, checks that patients will give informed consent, and monitors their safety. Without committee approval, the research cannot start.
