# Ethical Principles in Nursing

## Definition
**Ethical principles** are basic moral rules that guide nurses in making right decisions. They help protect patients and promote good care.

## Key Points
- The main ethical principles are autonomy, beneficence, non-maleficence, justice, fidelity, and veracity.
- These principles help nurses solve ethical problems.

## Detailed Explanation

1. **Autonomy** – Respect the patient's right to make their own decisions. The patient has the right to accept or refuse treatment.
   - Example: A patient has the right to refuse surgery.

2. **Beneficence** – Do good and promote the welfare of patients.
   - Example: Giving pain relief to a patient in pain.

3. **Non-maleficence** – Do no harm. Avoid actions that may hurt the patient.
   - Example: Checking a patient's allergy before giving medicine.

4. **Justice** – Treat all patients fairly and equally. No discrimination based on caste, religion, gender, or money.
   - Example: Giving emergency care to all patients equally.

5. **Fidelity** – Be faithful and keep promises to patients. Maintain trust.
   - Example: If a nurse promises to return with medicine, she must come back.

6. **Veracity** – Be truthful and honest with patients.
   - Example: Telling a patient the correct side effects of a medicine.

## Bullet Summary
- Autonomy = respect patient's choice
- Beneficence = do good
- Non-maleficence = do no harm
- Justice = fair treatment
- Fidelity = keep promises
- Veracity = tell the truth

## Example
A nurse explains all treatment options to a patient, respects the patient's decision, and gives safe care. This follows autonomy, beneficence, and non-maleficence.
