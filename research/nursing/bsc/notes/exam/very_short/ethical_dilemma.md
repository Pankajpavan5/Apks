# Ethical Dilemma

## Definition
An **ethical dilemma** is a situation where a person must choose between two or more options, and each option has both right and wrong aspects. It is a difficult choice between conflicting moral values.

## Key Points
- In an ethical dilemma, there is no clear right answer.
- Different ethical principles may conflict.
- Nurses often face ethical dilemmas in patient care.

## Detailed Explanation

### Common Causes
- Conflict between patient choice and medical advice
- Confidentiality vs. safety
- Family wishes vs. patient wishes
- Limited resources vs. patient need

### Examples in Nursing
1. A patient refuses life-saving treatment due to religious beliefs.
2. A family asks the nurse not to tell the patient about a cancer diagnosis.
3. There is only one ventilator and two patients need it.

### How to Resolve
1. Identify the problem clearly.
2. Gather all information.
3. Think about ethical principles involved.
4. Consider all options.
5. Consult with the healthcare team and ethics committee.
6. Choose the option that causes the least harm and respects patient rights.

## Bullet Summary
- Ethical dilemma = conflict between two morally right choices
- Causes: patient choice, confidentiality, family wishes, limited resources
- Resolution: gather facts, consider principles, consult team, choose least harmful option

## Example
A pregnant woman refuses blood transfusion because of her faith. The doctor believes it is needed to save her life. The nurse faces an ethical dilemma between respecting autonomy and doing good (beneficence).
