# Professional Value

## Definition
**Professional value** refers to the worth and importance of being a member of a profession. It includes the respect, dignity, and social recognition given to professionals because of their knowledge, skills, and service to society.

## Key Points
- Professional value means the positive qualities of a profession.
- It comes from education, ethics, and service.
- Nursing has professional value because nurses save lives and promote health.

## Detailed Explanation

### Sources of Professional Value
1. **Education** – Long and specialized training.
2. **Skill** – Ability to perform important healthcare tasks.
3. **Knowledge** – Understanding of health and disease.
4. **Ethics** – Commitment to moral behavior.
5. **Service** – Helping society and individuals.
6. **Responsibility** – Accountability for patient care.

### Why Nursing Has Professional Value
- Nurses care for the sick, weak, and vulnerable.
- Nursing requires scientific knowledge and practical skills.
- Nurses work in hospitals, communities, schools, and homes.
- Nurses promote health, prevent disease, and support recovery.

## Bullet Summary
- Professional value = worth and dignity of a profession
- Sources: education, skill, knowledge, ethics, service, responsibility
- Nursing has high professional value because of its service to society.
