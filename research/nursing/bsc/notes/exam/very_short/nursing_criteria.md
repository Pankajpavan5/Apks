# Nursing Criteria

## Definition
**Nursing criteria** are the standards or requirements used to judge good nursing practice and education.

## Key Points
- Criteria help evaluate nursing care and nursing programs.
- They ensure quality and safety in nursing practice.
- Criteria may include knowledge, skills, ethics, and attitudes.

## Detailed Explanation

Common criteria for nursing include:
1. **Knowledge** – Understanding nursing science, anatomy, physiology, and diseases.
2. **Skills** – Ability to perform procedures like injections, wound dressing, and vital signs monitoring.
3. **Attitude** – Professional behavior, empathy, and respect for patients.
4. **Ethics** – Following the nursing code of ethics.
5. **Competence** – Ability to provide safe and effective care.
6. **Communication** – Clear and respectful communication with patients and team.

## Bullet Summary
- Nursing criteria = standards to judge nursing practice/education
- Includes: knowledge, skills, attitude, ethics, competence, communication
- Used to maintain quality patient care.
