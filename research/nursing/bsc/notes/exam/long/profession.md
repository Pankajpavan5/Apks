# Profession

## Definition
A **profession** is an occupation that requires specialized education, training, and skill. It is based on a body of knowledge and serves society with a specific code of ethics and standards.

## Key Points
- A profession is different from a job or trade because it needs long formal education.
- Members of a profession have a social responsibility.
- Professions are regulated by codes of ethics and standards.
- Nursing is considered a profession because it has its own knowledge base, education, ethics, and service to society.

## Detailed Explanation
A profession has these important features:

1. **Specialized Knowledge** – A profession is based on a well-defined body of knowledge learned through formal education.
2. **Education and Training** – Long period of study and practical training is required.
3. **Service Orientation** – The main goal is to serve people and society, not only to earn money.
4. **Code of Ethics** – Professionals follow ethical rules to protect clients and society.
5. **Autonomy** – Professionals can make independent decisions within their field.
6. **Accountability** – Professionals are responsible for their actions.
7. **Professional Organization** – Professions have associations that set standards and support members.
8. **Continuing Education** – Professionals keep learning throughout their career.

## Bullet Summary
- Profession = specialized knowledge + formal education + ethics + service
- Features: knowledge, education, service, ethics, autonomy, accountability, organization, lifelong learning
- Nursing is a profession because it has all these features.

## Example
A nurse is a professional because she has studied nursing science, follows the Indian Nursing Council Code of Ethics, is accountable for patient care, and is a member of the Trained Nurses' Association of India (TNI).
