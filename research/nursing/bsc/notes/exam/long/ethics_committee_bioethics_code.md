# Ethics, Ethical Committee, Bioethics, and Code of Ethics

## Definition
- **Ethics** is the study of what is right and wrong in human behavior. It helps people decide the morally correct action.
- **Bioethics** is the study of ethical issues in healthcare, biology, and life sciences.
- **Ethical Committee** is a group of people who review and guide ethical decisions, especially in research and patient care.
- **Code of Ethics** is a written set of rules that tells professionals how to behave.

## Key Points
- Ethics guides correct behavior.
- Bioethics deals with moral problems in healthcare.
- Ethical committees protect patients and research participants.
- Code of ethics gives rules for professional conduct.

## Detailed Explanation

### 1. Ethics
Ethics comes from the Greek word "ethos" meaning character or custom. It deals with moral principles like:
- Right and wrong
- Good and bad
- Justice and fairness
- Duty and responsibility

In nursing, ethics helps nurses make decisions that respect patients and protect their welfare.

### 2. Bioethics
Bioethics applies ethics to healthcare and life sciences. It includes issues like:
- Patient consent
- End-of-life care
- Organ donation
- Abortion
- Genetic testing
- Research on humans

### 3. Ethical Committee
An ethical committee (also called Ethics Committee or Institutional Ethics Committee) reviews:
- Research proposals involving humans or animals
- Patient care decisions when there is confusion
- Cases of ethical dilemmas

Members usually include:
- Doctors
- Nurses
- Lawyers
- Social workers
- Community members
- Religious leaders

Functions:
- Protect patient rights
- Review research proposals
- Give ethical guidance
- Solve ethical dilemmas

### 4. Code of Ethics
A code of ethics is a written guide for professionals. It tells members:
- How to behave
- What their duties are
- How to treat clients
- What to do in difficult situations

The Indian Nursing Council (INC) Code of Ethics gives rules for nurses in India.

## Bullet Summary
- Ethics = moral principles of right and wrong
- Bioethics = ethics in healthcare and life sciences
- Ethical Committee = group that reviews ethical issues in research and care
- Code of Ethics = written rules for professional behavior
- All four protect patients, society, and professional standards.

## Example
When a hospital wants to start a new drug trial on patients, the Ethical Committee reviews the plan. It checks informed consent, patient safety, and privacy. The nurse follows the Code of Ethics while caring for the trial participants.
