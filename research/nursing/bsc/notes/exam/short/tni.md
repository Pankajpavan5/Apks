# TNI – Trained Nurses' Association of India

## Definition
**TNI** stands for the **Trained Nurses' Association of India**. It is a national professional organization for nurses in India.

## Key Points
- Founded in 1908 (earlier as the Association of Nursing Superintendents).
- It is one of the oldest nursing organizations in India.
- It works for the welfare and professional growth of nurses.

## Detailed Explanation

### Objectives
1. **Promote nursing education** – Improve the quality of nursing education in India.
2. **Professional development** – Provide training, workshops, and conferences for nurses.
3. **Welfare of nurses** – Protect the rights and interests of nurses.
4. **Standards of care** – Help maintain high standards of nursing practice.
5. **Publication** – Publishes the Indian Journal of Nursing and Midwifery (IJNM).
6. **Networking** – Connects nurses across India.

### Activities
- Organizing conferences and seminars
- Offering continuing education programs
- Publishing nursing journals
- Representing nurses in government committees
- Supporting nurses' welfare and professional rights

### Importance
- TNI gives nurses a united voice.
- It helps improve the status of nursing in India.
- It encourages research and evidence-based practice.
