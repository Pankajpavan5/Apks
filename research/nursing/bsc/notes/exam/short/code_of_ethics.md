# Code of Ethics in Nursing

## Definition
A **Code of Ethics** is a written set of rules that guides the behavior of nurses. It tells nurses what is right and wrong in professional practice.

## Key Points
- The Indian Nursing Council (INC) Code of Ethics is used in India.
- It protects patients and maintains professional standards.
- It includes duties towards patients, society, profession, and coworkers.

## Detailed Explanation

The Code of Ethics for nurses includes:
1. **Respect for patients** – Treat every patient with dignity and respect their privacy.
2. **Compassion and care** – Provide kind and considerate care to all patients.
3. **Honesty** – Be truthful in all professional activities.
4. **Confidentiality** – Do not share patient information without permission.
5. **Accountability** – Take responsibility for your nursing actions.
6. **Competence** – Practice only within your knowledge and skills.
7. **Justice** – Treat all patients fairly, without discrimination.
8. **Professional loyalty** – Support and maintain the dignity of the nursing profession.

## Bullet Summary
- Code of Ethics = written rules for nurses
- Made by: Indian Nursing Council (INC)
- Covers: respect, compassion, honesty, confidentiality, accountability, competence, justice, loyalty
- Purpose: protect patients and guide nurses

## Example
If a nurse learns that a patient has HIV, she must not tell anyone except the healthcare team involved in care. This is confidentiality as per the Code of Ethics.
