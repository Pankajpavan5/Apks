# Professional Values in Nursing

## Definition
**Professional values** are the beliefs and principles that guide nurses in their work. They help nurses decide what is right and important in patient care.

## Key Points
- Professional values shape nursing behavior.
- They are learned during nursing education and practice.
- They include caring, honesty, respect, responsibility, and justice.

## Detailed Explanation

Important professional values in nursing:

1. **Caring** – Showing concern and kindness for patients.
2. **Compassion** – Understanding and sharing the feelings of patients.
3. **Respect** – Honoring patients' dignity, culture, and choices.
4. **Honesty** – Being truthful with patients and colleagues.
5. **Accountability** – Accepting responsibility for one's actions.
6. **Justice** – Treating all patients fairly.
7. **Integrity** – Following moral principles even when difficult.
8. **Altruism** – Putting patient needs before personal comfort.
9. **Excellence** – Trying to give the best possible care.
10. **Autonomy** – Respecting patients' right to make their own decisions.

## Bullet Summary
- Professional values = beliefs that guide nursing behavior
- Main values: caring, compassion, respect, honesty, accountability, justice, integrity, altruism, excellence, autonomy
- These values help build trust between nurses and patients.
